Sheikh Hamza Yusuf

The man who persued knowledge from round the world

Summarization

Sheikh Hamza Yusuf, born Mark Hanson in America 1958, is a prominent Islamic Scholar. His parents were originally christians. But his mother used to take young Sheikh Hamza to different places of worship like churches and even once went into a mosque.

He likes to read books and read so many books when he was young.

Sheikh Hamza really started thinking about life after death when he had what he called a near death experience from a fatal accident and he eventually converted to be a muslim and pursued islamic knowledge to his utmost capacity.

Click here to watch a video of an interview of Sheikh Hamza by Jordan Peterson a canadian psychologist. You will be in awe of this man's indept knowledge of Islam and even psychology. You will literally be amazed by him.

Achievements

  • He is a co-founder of Zaituna College.
  • He is an advisor to both the Center for Islamic Studies at the Graduate Theological Union in Berkeley and the Islamic Studies programme at Stanford University.
  • He serves as vice-president for the Global Center for Guidance and Renewal, which was founded and is currently presided over by Abdallah bin Bayyah, his Mauritanian teacher.
  • He has high fluency in language especially classical Arabic.
  • Yusuf has been listed in the top 50 of The 500 Most Influential Muslims publication.

Conclusion

To conclude, I want to quote one of Sheikh Hamza's long Sayings:


"People say to you, 'you've changed', or something like that, well, I hope, for the sake of God that you have changed, because I don't want to be the same person all my life. I want to be growing, I want to be expanding. I want to be changing. Because animate things change, inanimate things don't change. Dead things don't change. And the heart should be alive, it should be changing, it should be moving, it should be growing, its knowledge should be expanding." - Shaykh Hamza Yusuf
